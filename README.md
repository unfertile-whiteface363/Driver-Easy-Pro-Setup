## 🛡️ Troubleshooting Common Issues

If you encounter trouble, review these common solutions.

- Application does not open: Ensure you have administrator rights on your computer. Right-click the icon and choose Run as administrator.
- Scan fails: Verify your internet connection. A stable connection ensures the tool reaches the update database.
- Driver fails to install: Sometimes a background process blocks the installation. Close other open programs and try the update again.
- Device does not work: If a specific hardware piece acts up after an update, open the Settings menu within the app and use the restore feature. This returns your driver to the version you had before.

## 💡 Frequently Asked Questions

What does WHQL mean? 
WHQL stands for Windows Hardware Quality Labs. These drivers pass testing by Microsoft to ensure compatibility with your Windows operating system.

Does the tool store my personal files? 
No. The software only reads hardware information to match your parts with the correct software updates.

How often should I scan? 
A scan once a month is sufficient for most users. If you play games or use heavy software, a scan every two weeks helps keep your graphics and sound hardware running smoothly.

Do I need a product key? 
The tool manages your updates efficiently. Follow the prompts in the interface for any registration steps required to access advanced features.

Is this safe for my computer? 
Yes. The software uses official driver packages. It avoids third-party or untested files to keep your system secure.
